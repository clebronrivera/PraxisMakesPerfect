## I. Current Coverage by Praxis Content Topic

### A. Data-based Decision-Making

#### 1. Problem identification

**Praxis Topics:**
- a. Understands various methods of information gathering (e.g., record review, interview strategies, observations, and testing [RIOT])
- b. Understands appropriate use of background information (e.g., student records, medical records and reports, reviews of previous interventions, developmental history)
- c. Understands appropriate use and interpretation of screening measures and methods

**Current Coverage:**
- ✅ **DBDM-S09**: Universal Screening Purpose (2 questions)
  - Covers screening purpose and interpretation
  - **Gap:** Does not cover RIOT framework, record review strategies, interview methods, or background information use

**Opportunities:**
- ❌ **RIOT Framework Application** - No coverage of the systematic approach to information gathering (Record review, Interview, Observation, Testing)
- ❌ **Background Information Use** - No coverage of how to appropriately use student records, medical records, developmental history
- ❌ **Interview Strategies** - No coverage of effective interview techniques for data collection
- ❌ **Observation Methods** - No coverage of systematic observation strategies
- ❌ **Record Review Protocols** - No coverage of how to review and synthesize information from records

**Recommended New Skills:**
1. **DBDM-S11**: RIOT Framework Application
   - Description: Apply the RIOT framework to systematically gather information through Record review, Interview, Observation, and Testing
   - DOK Range: 2-3

2. **DBDM-S12**: Background Information Integration
   - Description: Understand appropriate use and interpretation of background information (records, medical reports, developmental history) in assessment
   - DOK Range: 2-3

#### 2. Assessment and problem analysis

**Praxis Topics:**
- a. Understands theories of intelligence and the appropriate use and interpretation of measures of intellectual/cognitive functioning
- b. Understands appropriate use and interpretation of measures of educational achievement
- c. Knows appropriate use and interpretation of diagnostic/processing measures (e.g., memory, executive functioning, phonemic awareness)
- d. Understands appropriate use and interpretation of measures of affective/social/emotional functioning and behavior
- e. Knows appropriate use and interpretation of a functional behavioral assessment
- f. Understands appropriate use and interpretation of performance-based assessment (e.g., work samples, portfolios)
- g. Understands appropriate use and interpretation of curriculum-based assessment/curriculum-based measures
- h. Knows appropriate use and interpretation of ecological assessment (e.g., classroom, family, community characteristics)
- i. Knows how to use information and technology resources to enhance data collection and decision making
- j. Understands the use of ongoing data collection to systematically assess the quality and effectiveness of academic, mental health, and system-level services

**Current Coverage:**
- ✅ **NEW-1-IQvsAchievement**: Intelligence vs. Achievement Distinction (1 question)
  - Covers basic distinction between intelligence and achievement tests
- ✅ **NEW-1-PerformanceAssessment**: Performance-Based Assessment Recognition (1 question)
  - Covers performance-based assessments (portfolios, work samples)
- ✅ **DBDM-S08**: Progress Monitoring Protocol (4 questions)
  - Covers ongoing data collection for progress monitoring
  - **Partial coverage** of topic 2.j (ongoing data collection)

**Opportunities:**
- ❌ **Theories of Intelligence** - No coverage of different intelligence theories (e.g., CHC theory, multiple intelligences) and their implications for assessment
- ❌ **Cognitive Functioning Measures** - Limited coverage; only basic IQ vs. achievement distinction
- ❌ **Educational Achievement Measures** - No specific coverage of achievement test selection and interpretation
- ❌ **Diagnostic/Processing Measures** - **Major gap:** No coverage of memory, executive functioning, phonemic awareness, or other processing assessments
- ❌ **Affective/Social/Emotional Measures** - No coverage of measures for emotional/behavioral functioning
- ❌ **Functional Behavioral Assessment** - **Major gap:** No coverage of FBA interpretation (Note: FBA is covered in Domain 4, but interpretation/use in data-based decision making could be in Domain 1)
- ❌ **Curriculum-Based Assessment/Measures** - **Major gap:** No specific coverage of CBA/CBM beyond progress monitoring
- ❌ **Ecological Assessment** - **Major gap:** No coverage of classroom, family, or community assessment
- ❌ **Technology Resources** - No coverage of using technology to enhance data collection

**Recommended New Skills:**
1. **DBDM-S13**: Intelligence Theory Application
   - Description: Understand different theories of intelligence and their implications for test selection and interpretation
   - DOK Range: 2-3

2. **DBDM-S14**: Cognitive Processing Assessment
   - Description: Select and interpret diagnostic/processing measures (memory, executive functioning, phonemic awareness, processing speed)
   - DOK Range: 3-4

3. **DBDM-S15**: Achievement Test Selection & Interpretation
   - Description: Select appropriate achievement measures and interpret results in context of student needs
   - DOK Range: 2-3

4. **DBDM-S16**: Affective/Social/Emotional Assessment
   - Description: Select and interpret measures of affective, social, and emotional functioning
   - DOK Range: 2-3

5. **DBDM-S17**: Curriculum-Based Assessment Application
   - Description: Understand appropriate use and interpretation of CBA/CBM beyond progress monitoring (screening, diagnostic purposes)
   - DOK Range: 2-3

6. **DBDM-S18**: Ecological Assessment Application
   - Description: Conduct and interpret ecological assessments of classroom, family, and community factors affecting student performance
   - DOK Range: 3-4

7. **DBDM-S19**: Technology-Enhanced Data Collection
   - Description: Use information and technology resources to enhance data collection and decision-making
   - DOK Range: 2-3

#### 3. Knowledge of measurement theory and principles

**Praxis Topics:**
- a. Knows how to use a problem-solving framework as the basis for all professional activities (e.g., Multitiered System of Supports, Response to Intervention)
- b. Understands the use and interpretation of different types of test scores and norms (e.g., grade- and age-referenced)
- c. Knows the strengths and limitations of various types of assessment procedures (e.g., self-report tests and inventories, multiple-choice tests, interviews)
- d. Knows the principles of reliability and validity
- e. Knows personal, social, linguistic, environmental, racial, and cultural factors that may influence assessment procedures
- f. Knows about test fairness and equity concepts (e.g., implicit bias, explicit bias)

**Current Coverage:**
- ✅ **DBDM-S01**: Reliability Type Selection (3 questions)
  - Covers reliability principles and types
- ✅ **DBDM-S02**: Validity Type Recognition (1 question)
  - Covers validity principles and types
- ✅ **DBDM-S03**: Score Interpretation (12 questions)
  - Covers test scores, norms, standard scores, confidence intervals
  - **Strong coverage** of topic 3.b (test scores and norms)
- ✅ **DBDM-S10**: Data-First Decision Making (4 questions)
  - Covers problem-solving framework application
  - **Partial coverage** of topic 3.a (problem-solving framework)

**Opportunities:**
- ❌ **Problem-Solving Framework Depth** - Current coverage is basic; could expand to cover MTSS/RTI as data-based decision-making frameworks
- ❌ **Assessment Procedure Strengths/Limitations** - **Major gap:** No coverage of when to use self-report vs. multiple-choice vs. interviews, and their respective limitations
- ❌ **Factors Influencing Assessment** - **Major gap:** No coverage of personal, social, linguistic, environmental, racial, and cultural factors affecting assessment
- ❌ **Test Fairness and Equity** - **Major gap:** No coverage of implicit/explicit bias, fairness concepts, or equitable assessment practices

**Recommended New Skills:**
1. **DBDM-S20**: Problem-Solving Framework Application (MTSS/RTI)
   - Description: Apply MTSS/RTI problem-solving framework as basis for data-based decision-making
   - DOK Range: 3-4

2. **DBDM-S21**: Assessment Procedure Selection
   - Description: Understand strengths and limitations of different assessment procedures (self-report, multiple-choice, interviews) and select appropriately
   - DOK Range: 2-3

3. **DBDM-S22**: Factors Influencing Assessment
   - Description: Recognize and account for personal, social, linguistic, environmental, racial, and cultural factors that may influence assessment procedures and results
   - DOK Range: 3-4

4. **DBDM-S23**: Test Fairness and Equity
   - Description: Understand test fairness and equity concepts, including implicit/explicit bias, and apply to assessment selection and interpretation
   - DOK Range: 3-4

   - DOK Range: 3-4

3. **DBDM-S26**: Low-Incidence Exceptionalities Assessment
