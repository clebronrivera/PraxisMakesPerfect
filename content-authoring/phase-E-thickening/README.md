# Stage 4 Content Thickening: Domain I (Professional Practices)

## Overview

This folder contains thickened, richer content drafts for all 16 modules in Praxis 5403 Domain I: Professional Practices (NASP Domains 1 & 2: Data-Based Decision Making + Consultation/Collaboration).

**Goal:** Transform thin module prose (32–92 words per current module) into teaching-level content (700–1200 words per thickened module) that a Praxis candidate can actually study and learn from.

**Scope:** 16 modules
- **Domain 1 (Data-Based Decision Making):** 12 modules (MOD-D1-01 through MOD-D1-12)
- **Domain 2 (Consultation/Collaboration):** 4 modules (MOD-D2-01 through MOD-D2-04)

---

## What "Thickened" Means

Each module now contains:

1. **In Plain Language** — An 80–120 word opener explaining the concept as you'd tell a colleague at lunch, with a concrete anchor example.

2. **First-Principles Explanation** — 150–250 words exploring *why* this rule, concept, or framework exists and what underlying logic test-writers are probing.

3. **Worked Example** — A 100–150 word case walked through step-by-step (a narrative, not Q&A). For assessment modules, this shows a student's scores and interpretation. For consultation modules, it shows a real dialogue or scenario.

4. **Named Misconceptions** — 1–3 explicit student traps: "Students often confuse X with Y because __. The difference is __."

5. **If You Only Remember One Thing** — A 25–40 word anchor that captures the single test-winning insight.

6. **Mnemonic or Analogy** — Where appropriate, a memorable device. If no clean analogy exists, this section is skipped (do not force).

7. **Proposed Visual Primitive** — A description (in words) of what visual should accompany the concept (comparison table, pyramid, flowchart, timeline, etc.). The visual is not built here; it is specified for later design/development.

8. **3 Concept-Specific Mini-Quiz Stems** — New question seeds (not from the current question bank). Just the stem and correct answer; distractors can be added later by authors.

9. **Concept Tags** — 3–8 short tags for Stage 1 `concepts` field (e.g., "FERPA", "Confidentiality-exceptions", "Parental-consent").

## How to Review These Drafts

1. **Read in order:** D1-01 → D1-12 → D2-01 → D2-04. The conceptual threads build.

2. **Check accuracy first:** Verify all claims against NASP 2020, DSM-5, and clinical practice. Flag with [VERIFY:...] as needed.

3. **Assess depth:** Does each module teach the concept or just summarize it? Original modules were summaries; thickened modules should explain *why* and show *how*.

4. **Verify worked examples:** Do the cases feel realistic? Are they specific enough to teach?

5. **Check misconceptions:** Are the traps real? Will test-takers actually fall into them?

6. **Test the "one thing":** Can a student memorize the "If You Only Remember One Thing" and answer most Praxis questions on that topic?

7. **Visual primitives:** Are the visual recommendations clear enough that a designer could implement them? Do they match the concept?

8. **Quiz stems:** Are the 3 stems aligned to the module and distinct from existing question bank?
