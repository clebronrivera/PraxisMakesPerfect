What I changed here

Each stat card has a color-stripe top.

Peach/mint/ice/lavender at the top edge, with a soft glow. You can scan the row and know which domain or category each number belongs to.

Baseline ghost + goal marker on every bar.

One filled bar (current), one faint vertical line (baseline), one mint vertical line (80% goal). Replaces the three overlapping elements in the current design.

Skill dots use short codes, not just color.

Each dot shows its skill code ("L2", "P4") in miniature. Same color semantics as before (rose=emerging, peach=approaching, mint=demonstrating), but now you can point and say "what's L2".

Top errors are narrative, not just numbers.

Each of the 5 includes a one-liner diagnosis: "treating FAPE as optional when the child has a 504," "confusing non-discriminatory assessment with accommodations." The report teaches, not just reports.

Assumption badges are inline, not hidden.

Every post-diagnostic number (streak, growth, study time) carries an ASSUMED chip. Once practice data populates, the chips disappear on their own.

Advanced analytics is a 2×2 grid of its own.

Confidence, time, knowledge type, distractor patterns — each in a small glass card with a short interpretive line at the bottom. No collapsible drawer anymore.
