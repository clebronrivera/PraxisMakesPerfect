Four takes on the brain motif and one alternative (the cochlea — the ear of "listens"). Each tile is 1:1 and sits on the same navy backdrop the hero uses. Pick the approach you want, or tell me to mash two together.

No. 01 · revised

Anatomical lateral

Proper lobed silhouette — protruding frontal pole, hanging temporal lobe, distinctly separate cerebellum with visible folia, brainstem stalk. Reads as a lateral-view brain at a glance.

No. 02

Editorial line drawing

Ink-only, no fills — like a botanical plate or mid-century medical illustration. Reads as a brain instantly. Domain colors reduced to four tiny pins on the lobes.

No. 03

Top-down symmetric

Looking down at both hemispheres. Central longitudinal fissure is the strong vertical axis; four colored regions sit in quadrants (frontal L/R, parietal L/R). Reads as a brain from the classic anatomical angle.

No. 04

Neural constellation

Brain silhouette made of neurons connected by synapses, with four domain-colored "anchor" neurons that light up on hover. More abstract — reads as "networked intelligence" first, "brain" second.

No. 05 · Alternative

Cochlear spiral

Not a brain — the ear of "A study plan that listens." A golden-spiral cochlea with four domain pins on the outermost coil. Tightest conceptual fit to the headline; cleanest form on the page.

No. 06 · Hybrid

Ink + neural firing

Study 02's editorial line drawing, but with the four cortex regions lit softly behind the ink. Keeps the "this is clearly a brain" legibility while letting the domain colors shine. My pick for best-of-both.

No. 07 · Iconic

Two-lobe walnut

The brain everyone recognizes — two rounded hemispheres joined by a deep central fissure, wavy walnut-fold gyri on each side, cerebellum peeking below. Sacrifices anatomical accuracy for instant legibility. This is the "🧠 emoji" rendered editorially.
