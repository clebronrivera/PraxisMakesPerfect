VIII Tactical Instructions

Rules of engagement.

Eight coaching rules tailored to your specific diagnostic shape. Skim them once; they'll surface again in context.

1

Vocab-first, always. Never start a case-practice session without 10 minutes of vocabulary in the same cluster. Vocabulary was the bottleneck across your 11 skills at 0%.

2

Cap legal at 90 min/day. It's your weakest cluster; more than 90 min/day triggers fatigue errors that look like progress but erase the next day.

3

Re-test Ethics every 4 days. Spaced review only, not re-practice. Your 100% there is fragile without rehearsal.

4

Fast + wrong ⇒ stop. If you answer wrong in under 5 seconds, that's a vocabulary miss, not a reasoning miss. Switch to the AI Tutor for a concept card before you continue.

5

Checkpoint on Saturdays. Take the 20-minute mixed quiz. If target-cluster accuracy dips below 70%, add a reinforcement week before moving on.

6

Procedural-only distractors are your tell. You over-select "procedural" answers when a substantive one is correct (38% of your wrong answers). When two answers look right, pick the more substantive one.

7

End every day with one redemption round. 10 minutes, no more. Keeps quarantine tight without cannibalizing new learning.

8

No mock exam until Week 6. Earlier full-lengths will pattern-match to your gaps and demoralize. Trust the weekly checkpoints.

For your review · Layout decision

Tabbed vs. Document — which wins?

Both are working mockups. If you scroll this far, the document version probably feels more like reading a plan than clicking through one.

Tabbed (v1)

Open v1 →

Wins: compact, dashboard-like, fast re-entry to a specific section.

Loses: users never see the plan as a whole. PDF mapping is messy — you have to choose which tabs become which pages. Mobile is cramped.

Fit: good for a returning user who wants "just the weekly plan." Bad for a first read.

Document (v2 · this page)

Recommended

Wins: reads like a plan. 1:1 PDF mapping. Sticky ToC keeps navigation without fragmenting content. Mobile scrolls naturally.

Loses: first-render height is ~4,000px — can feel heavy if you just want one stat.

Fit: matches the actual nature of the artifact. Makes the PDF download meaningful instead of an afterthought.

Design decisions worth calling out

1

Fraunces serif for chapter titles.

A quiet serif next to Inter for body. Gives the document real "plan" weight without feeling academic.
2

Roman numerals for chapter markers.

I, II, III … IX. A small cue that this is a document, not a SaaS page with tabs.
3

Case patterns as tiny decision trees.

Monospaced mini-trees show the shape of each archetype. They print cleanly in the PDF too.
4

Sticky ToC with page numbers.

Each entry shows which PDF page it corresponds to. Reinforces the document metaphor.
5

Tactical instructions are numbered italic serifs.

Coaching voice, not bullet points. Feels like the "author's note" section of a study guide.
6

Checkpoint gates are typed.

"Required," "Soft," "Gate," "Final" — so users know which ones stop them and which ones are advisory.
