Vocabulary

4 of 20 shown

Prior Written Notice (PWN)

Written notice a school must give parents before it proposes — or refuses — to initiate or change identification, evaluation, placement, or FAPE.

LEG-02 ▾
Why it mattersAlmost every IDEA vignette hinges on whether PWN was properly issued. A missing PWN is a due-process defect.
Shows up inLEG-02 (IDEA & FAPE), LEG-03 (Evaluation Procedures), disciplinary removal items.
Confusion riskOften confused with Procedural Safeguards Notice — PWN is triggered by a specific action; Procedural Safeguards is given annually.

FAPE

Free Appropriate Public Education — the core right IDEA guarantees every eligible student.

LEG-02 ▾
Why it mattersTests treat FAPE as the substantive question; procedural moves are evaluated by whether they protect it.
Shows up inLEG-02, LEG-04 (Compliance), DIV-01 (Non-Discriminatory Assessment).
Confusion riskSection 504 also guarantees FAPE but under a different standard — equal access, not appropriate education.

Standard Error of Measurement (SEM)

The uncertainty band around a test score — reflects how much the score would fluctuate across repeated testing.

PSY-01 ▾
Why it mattersA point estimate near a cutoff is indeterminate once SEM is applied. Reporting a range prevents false classification.
Shows up inPSY-01 (Score Interpretation), PSY-02 (Reliability), any eligibility vignette with a borderline score.
Confusion riskOften confused with Standard Error of the Estimate (used in regression). SEM is score-level; SEE is prediction-level.

Non-Discriminatory Assessment

Using evaluation instruments and procedures that don't disadvantage a student based on race, culture, language, or disability.

PSY-04 · DIV-01 ▾
Why it mattersIDEA mandates it. Violations can invalidate an entire eligibility determination.
Shows up inPSY-04 (CLD Assessment), DIV-01 (Diversity), any eligibility vignette mentioning race or language.
Confusion riskDistinct from test accommodations — accommodations adjust test delivery; non-discriminatory means selecting or adapting the instrument itself.

Case patterns

4 of 8 shown

Parent refuses evaluation

Tests your grasp of due-process override vs. documenting + revisiting.

▾
What to notice
  • Parent withholds consent in writing
  • Student shows suspected disability but no immediate safety concern
  • School has completed pre-referral interventions
Common mistakeAssuming the school can unilaterally proceed. IDEA protects parent consent; override requires due process and is rare.

Disciplinary removal ≥ 10 days

Whether a manifestation determination is required and what the outcome triggers.

▾
What to notice
  • Student with IEP accumulates suspension days
  • Cumulative removal crosses the 10-day threshold
  • Principal proposes further removal or expulsion
Common mistakeCounting a single suspension, not the cumulative total. Or skipping the manifestation review entirely.

Score near cutoff, parent disagrees

Whether the score is indeterminate given measurement error, and what options the parent has.

▾
What to notice
  • Composite score within 1–2 points of eligibility cutoff
  • Parent requests the student qualify
  • Report does not mention SEM or CI
Common mistakeTreating the point estimate as definitive. Also: forgetting parents have the right to an Independent Educational Evaluation.

Bilingual CLD student referral

What must happen first — language dominance assessment — before cognitive or academic testing is defensible.

▾
What to notice
  • Spanish-dominant or bilingual student referred for suspected LD
  • Language dominance not yet established
  • Teacher reports academic concerns without language context
Common mistakeProceeding to English-normed cognitive testing, or selecting "translate the test" rather than "assess in the dominant language with a proctored interpreter."
