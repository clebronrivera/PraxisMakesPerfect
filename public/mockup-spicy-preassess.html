Correct!
Consultee-centered consultation · CON-01

A school psychologist utilizes consultee-centered consultation to help a teacher who lacks objectivity…

B ✓ To improve the teacher's ability to handle similar cases in the future by addressing their professional objectivity
Why This Is Correct

In consultee-centered consultation, the focus is on improving the consultee's (teacher's) ability to handle the current case AND similar future cases. When the consultee "lacks objectivity," the consultant works to identify and address the source of that objectivity loss — which could be:

  • • Theme interference — personal issues projected onto the professional situation
  • • Simple identification — over-identifying with the student
  • • Transference — reacting to the student based on past experiences
Why Not the Others?
A — Confuses with client-centered consultation

Direct behavior change targets the client (student), not the consultee. This describes client-centered consultation.

C — Confuses with behavioral consultation

Developing a BIP is the hallmark of behavioral consultation, which follows a structured problem-solving model.

D — Confuses with assessment role

Data collection for eligibility is an assessment function, not a consultation goal.

💡 Core Concept

Caplan's four consultation types are distinguished by who/what is the focus: client-centered (fix the student), consultee-centered (fix the teacher), program-centered (fix the system), and consultee-centered administrative (fix the administrator).
