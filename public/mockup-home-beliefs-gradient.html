Reliability & Validity Foundations ⚠ You were “Sure” on 3 of 4 — blind spot

The belief

A test that's reliable is therefore valid.

Actually

Reliability is consistency; validity is whether it measures the right thing. A bathroom scale can be perfectly reliable and still weigh the wrong quantity. They're independent.

Special Education Law Foundations

The belief

A 504 plan needs the same eligibility as an IEP.

Actually

Section 504 only requires a disability that substantially limits a major life activity — a far wider net than IDEA's 13 categories plus “adverse educational impact.” Many students qualify for 504 but not an IEP.

Functional Behavioral Assessment Professional Practices

The belief

I define the target behavior by what it looks like.

Actually

An FBA targets the function (escape, attention, access, sensory) — not the topography. “Throws pencil” isn't the unit; “escapes a hard task” is. Same behavior, different function, different plan.
